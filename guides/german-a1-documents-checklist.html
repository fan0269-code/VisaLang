Germany family reunion

German A1 documents checklist for family reunion

This checklist helps you keep German A1 exam booking records, certificate details, and visa-file language-proof items in one place. It is not the official appointment checklist. Use it to prepare better questions for the German mission, responsible authority, or official test center.

Quick answer

Your A1 document work has two parts: exam proof and visa-file proof.

For the exam, check ID, exam name, test center, fee, seat, result date, and certificate delivery. For the visa file, check whether the authority needs an original, copy, translation, upload, appointment-day document, or later submission. Do not assume the test-center booking confirmation is the same as accepted language proof.

Who this is for

Use this page if you are preparing German A1 proof for a Germany family reunion route, especially if you are booking Goethe A1, comparing Goethe A1 with telc A1, or trying to avoid a missing document at the visa appointment.

Exam booking checklist

  • Use the same name spelling as your passport or accepted ID.
  • Confirm the exact exam name and level before payment.
  • Save the official test-center URL and the date you checked it.
  • Record fee, currency, payment deadline, refund rule, and rescheduling rule.
  • Check whether the center requires printed confirmation, photos, or special arrival instructions.
  • Ask when results and certificates are normally issued.

Visa file checklist

Keep the language-proof record beside your appointment checklist. The authority may care about the certificate owner, level, issue date, original format, copies, spelling, and whether the proof is available before or after the appointment.

Document Risk to check Where to verify
A1 certificate Wrong level, wrong provider, delayed issue date, or name mismatch. German mission, responsible authority, exam owner.
Exam confirmation Booking confirmation may not replace final language proof. Appointment instructions and authority guidance.
Passport or ID Different spelling between exam registration and visa file. Test center and appointment checklist.
Translations or copies Some files may require a specific copy, upload, or original presentation rule. German mission checklist for your location.

Common mistakes

Most document problems are preventable if you check early.

Using a booking confirmation as if it were the certificate

A booking record proves you registered. It does not prove you passed or that the authority accepts the result.

Ignoring name spelling

Small differences in name order, spacing, accents, or passport spelling can cause confusion. Match official ID wherever possible.

Checking the checklist too late

If the appointment is soon, seat availability, result date, retake risk, and certificate delivery can all become urgent at once.
