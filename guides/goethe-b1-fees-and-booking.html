Germany B1+

Goethe B1 fees and booking by country

Goethe-Zertifikat B1 pricing is set locally by each exam centre, so this page is a fee-check and booking workflow — not a fixed price list. It covers the 5-step booking workflow, a fee-check checklist, a country comparison of what differs, rescheduling and refund rules, and the most common booking mistakes.

TL;DR verdict

Goethe-Institut does not publish a single global B1 fee — each institute and licensed test partner sets its own price, and the number moves with currency, centre policy, and sometimes the exam session. The reliable approach is: find your local centre on the Goethe-Institut locations page, open that centre's B1 exam page, record the fee with the date you checked it, and confirm the price with the centre directly before paying. Treat any fee number older than ~30 days as stale. Always confirm the fee, date, and accepted ID before booking.

The 5-step booking workflow

  1. Confirm the route needs B1. Before any booking, confirm with the German mission or registry that Goethe-Zertifikat B1 is on the accepted list for your specific visa or registration route. Booking before confirming risks paying for a certificate your case does not need.
  2. Find your local centre. Use the Goethe-Institut locations page to find your nearest institute or licensed exam partner. In countries without a Goethe-Institut, licensed partners run the same exams under Goethe's framework.
  3. Open the B1 page on that centre's site. Navigate to the exam section and locate Goethe-Zertifikat B1 (not A1, not C1). Note the fee, the next available dates, the registration deadline, and the payment method.
  4. Register and pay on the centre's page. Follow the centre's own registration flow — Goethe does not run a single global booking portal. Keep the confirmation email; it carries your date, time, and centre address.
  5. Confirm the details with the centre before the exam. Re-check the date, time, accepted ID, and any centre-specific rules (e.g. arrival window, allowed pens) about one week before. Centre policies can change between booking and test day.

Rescheduling, refunds & retakes

Rescheduling and refund rules are set by each centre, not by Goethe centrally. As a general pattern: centres allow you to move the exam date if you ask before a deadline (often ~7–14 days before the exam), but may charge a fee or refuse changes inside that window. Refunds for cancellation are usually partial and also time-limited. Confirm the exact deadlines and any rescheduling fee with your centre at booking — write them on the same fee-checklist page above.

Retakes are almost always a separate fee. If you fail one module (listening, reading, writing, or speaking), you usually retake just that module and pay for that module — but some centres require re-sitting the full exam. Confirm the retake policy before your first sitting so you can plan the budget and the timeline.
