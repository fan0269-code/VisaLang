Goethe B1 vs telc B1: which is accepted where

A side-by-side comparison of Goethe-Zertifikat B1 and telc Deutsch B1 — format, modules, centre network, the integration-course link, fees, and which authorities tend to accept which. This is orientation, not legal advice; always confirm with the authority handling your case before booking.

TL;DR verdict

Both Goethe-Zertifikat B1 and telc Deutsch B1 certify CEFR B1 and both are widely accepted for German settlement, naturalisation, and many work-registration routes — but they are issued by different bodies, their formats differ in detail, and acceptance is set by the authority handling your specific case, not by the exam providers. The single most useful step is to ask your Ausländerbehörde or Einbürgerungsbehörde for its accepted-certificate list before booking either. If you completed an integration course, your DTZ result may already satisfy the B1 condition and you may not need either.

Module-by-module format differences

The four modules are the same in name; the differences are in style and emphasis. Confirm the current format at your chosen centre — both providers update their test designs.

ModuleGoethe B1telc B1
Hören (Listening)Everyday dialogues, announcements; multiple-choice / matchingSimilar everyday input; item types may differ in detail
Lesen (Reading)Articles, emails, opinion texts; longer than A1Comparable; often includes job-related and everyday texts
Schreiben (Writing)Email/letter + opinion textEmail/letter + guided writing; prompts may lean practical

Neither is "easier" in general — learners with a presentation habit often prefer Goethe's short-talk format; learners who role-play conversations comfortably often prefer telc's paired speaking. Try a sample paper from each before deciding.

The integration-course shortcut (DTZ)

The biggest practical difference between the two providers is not the exam format — it is that telc operates the Deutsch-Test für Zuwanderer (DTZ), the exam sat at the end of a state-subsidised integration course. If you completed an integration course and your DTZ result certifies B1, you may already have the language proof your route requires and you may not need a separate Goethe B1 or telc B1 sitting at all.

Before booking either exam, check with your integration-course provider and the authority handling your case: (1) whether your DTZ result is on file and certifies B1, and (2) whether the authority accepts DTZ as the B1 proof for your route. If yes, you save the entire booking fee and study time of a separate sitting.

Fees & centre availability

Neither provider publishes a single global B1 fee — each centre sets its own price. As a rule of thumb: Goethe-Institut branches run regular sessions at institute-set prices; telc-licensed centres (often language schools) may offer more local dates and sometimes lower fees, but with more variation between centres. For the practical booking workflow and fee-check checklist, see our Goethe B1 fees and booking guide — the same workflow applies to a telc centre, just on the telc.net centre finder instead of goethe.de.
