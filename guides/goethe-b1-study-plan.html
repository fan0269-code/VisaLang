Germany B1+

60-day Goethe B1 study plan for working adults

A practical eight-week plan for the Goethe-Zertifikat B1 exam, built around a full-time work schedule — with weekly timetables, speaking templates, writing frames, and a weak-skill repair routine. It is a study aid, not a guarantee of passing and not a replacement for official Goethe practice materials.

TL;DR verdict

Goethe-Institut describes Goethe-Zertifikat B1 as certifying "independent language use" — the level where you can handle most everyday and travel situations, express opinions, and describe experiences. The four modules are the same as A1 — listening (Hören), reading (Lesen), writing (Schreiben), speaking (Sprechen) — but the input is longer, the topics broader (work, health, media, society), and the speaking test includes a short presentation. Sixty days is realistic for a working adult who can free up roughly 45–75 minutes on weekdays and 2–3 hours on weekends, and who already has A2-equivalent German. If you are starting from A1, plan for 4–6 months instead. Always confirm the current format, passing marks, and module weights on the official Goethe B1 page before you book.

Who this is for / who should skip

Best for

  • Working adults who need B1 for a German settlement (Niederlassungserlaubnis) or work-registration route and can free up 6–10 hours a week.
  • Learners already at A2 who want an 8-week structured revision plan before a booked Goethe B1 date.
  • Applicants whose employer allows lunch-break or commute study blocks and who can protect one weekend morning for a full mock.

Skip if

  • Your test is less than 21 days away — switch to official Goethe B1 mock papers and a 3-week crash routine instead of a 60-day plan.
  • You are still at A1 — jump to our 30-day A1 plan first; B1 builds on A2 vocabulary and grammar you do not have yet.
  • You have not confirmed with the German mission or registry (Bürgeramt / Ausländerbehörde) that Goethe B1 is accepted for your specific route — confirm first; see our Goethe B1 for settlement and work guide.

The 8-week plan (weekly tables)

Each week assumes 6–10 hours total: ~45–75 min on weekdays (split across commute, lunch, evening) and ~2–3 hours on one weekend morning. If you can do more, add official Goethe B1 practice papers; if less, compress the "optional" rows first.

Copyable templates — speaking & writing

Speaking Teil 1 — self-introduction & small talk frame

Guten Tag. Mein Name ist ______.
Ich bin ______ Jahre alt und komme aus ______.
Ich wohne seit ______ in ______ und arbeite als ______.
In meiner Freizeit ______ (Hobby / Sport / Familie).
Ich lerne Deutsch, weil ______ (Grund / Ziel).

Practise until you can deliver it in under 90 seconds without reading. Then prepare two follow-up sentences per prompt — examiners often ask a "Warum?" or "Wie finden Sie…?" right after the intro.

Speaking Teil 2 — topic presentation frame (2 minutes)

Mein Thema ist ______.
Zuerst möchte ich sagen, dass ______.
Außerdem ist ______ wichtig, weil ______.
Ein Beispiel dafür ist ______.
Zum Schluss finde ich, dass ______.

Use exactly five sentences for the talk; this keeps you inside the time limit and prevents the most common B1 error — running over and being cut off.

Writing — formal email frame

Betreff: ______
Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren,
ich schreibe Ihnen wegen ______.
Ich möchte ______ (Anfrage / Beschwerde / Termin).
Können Sie mir ______ mitteilen?
Vielen Dank im Voraus.
Mit freundlichen Grüßen
______ (Ihr Name)

This frame fits the most common B1 writing prompts: a request, a complaint, a booking, or asking for information. Always fill the Betreff — a missing subject line is a frequent point loss at B1 just as at A1.

Documents checklist (week 8)

  • Valid ID / passport (name matches the booking exactly).
  • Booking confirmation email from Goethe-Institut.
  • Confirmation of fee payment (printed or on phone).
  • Pencils and eraser as allowed by your test centre.
  • Arrival 20–30 minutes before the start time; plan commute delay.

Fitting study around a full-time job

The biggest risk for working adults is not the exam difficulty — it is consistency. Three patterns that work in practice:

  • Commute block: 20 minutes of listening or vocabulary on the way to work. Pair it with a fixed podcast (e.g. DW Langsam gesprochene Nachrichten) so you do not spend willpower choosing content.
  • Lunch block: 15 minutes of reading or grammar drill. Keep one printed B1 reader or a saved article folder so the start-up cost is zero.
  • Weekend mock: protect one weekend morning (Saturday or Sunday) for a 2–3 hour block — one full module mock plus review. This is where real progress happens; weekday blocks only keep the level stable.

Avoid the trap of "I will study when I have a free evening." Free evenings rarely arrive on schedule; the three fixed blocks above are how 60-day plans actually complete.

Goethe B1 vs telc B1 — short comparison

Both Goethe-Zertifikat B1 and telc Deutsch B1 sit at CEFR B1 and are widely accepted for settlement and certain work routes, but they are issued by different bodies and have different formats. The table is a quick orientation only — verify acceptance with the authority handling your route.
