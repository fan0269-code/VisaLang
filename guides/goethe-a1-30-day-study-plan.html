30-day Goethe A1 study plan

A practical four-week plan for the Goethe-Zertifikat A1: Start Deutsch 1 exam — with weekly timetables, speaking-topic templates, writing frames, and a documents checklist. It is a study aid, not a guarantee of passing and not a replacement for official Goethe practice materials.

TL;DR verdict

Goethe-Institut describes Goethe-Zertifikat A1: Start Deutsch 1 as a certificate proving "very basic language skills" on the CEFR A1 level, with four modules — listening (Hören), reading (Lesen), writing (Schreiben), and speaking (Sprechen). Thirty days is realistic if you can commit roughly 60–90 minutes a day and you already know the Latin alphabet. If you are starting from a non-Latin script (e.g. Arabic or Cyrillic) or have no German at all, plan for 6–8 weeks instead and use this plan's Week 1 twice. Always confirm the current exam format and passing marks on the official Goethe A1 page before you book.

Who this is for / who should skip

Best for

  • Spouses joining a partner in Germany who need A1 proof for the family-reunion visa and have 4–6 weeks before a test date.
  • Self-learners who can study 60–90 minutes daily and want a structured weekly checklist rather than a generic textbook.
  • Applicants who already have a Goethe test date booked and need a final-month revision plan.

Skip if

  • Your test is less than 14 days away — focus on official mock papers instead of a 30-day plan.
  • You are targeting Goethe B1 for settlement or work — the B1 format and vocabulary load are materially different; see our Goethe B1 study plan.
  • Your consulate has not yet confirmed which certificates it accepts — confirm the accepted-exam list with the German mission first, then return here.

Copyable templates — speaking & writing

Speaking Teil 1 — self-introduction frame

Guten Tag. Ich heiße ______. Ich komme aus ______.
Ich wohne in ______. Ich bin ______ Jahre alt.
Ich bin von Beruf ______. Meine Muttersprache ist ______.
Ich lerne Deutsch seit ______ Monaten.

Replace the blanks, then practise until you can deliver it in under 45 seconds without reading. Avoid over-rehearsed answers — examiners notice and it limits the follow-up conversation.

Speaking Teil 2 — five WH-questions to prepare

  • Wer ist in Ihrer Familie? (Who is in your family?)
  • Wo wohnen Sie? (Where do you live?)
  • Was machen Sie beruflich? (What is your job?)
  • Wann lernen Sie Deutsch? (When do you learn German?)
  • Wie fahren Sie zur Arbeit / zum Kurs? (How do you travel to work / class?)

Writing — 5-line short-email frame

Betreff: ______
Hallo ______,
ich ______ (was / wann / wo). Können Sie ______?
Vielen Dank! / Viele Grüße
______ (Dein Name)

This frame covers the most common A1 writing prompts: an invitation, a short request, or confirming an appointment. Always fill the subject line (Betreff) — a missing Betreff is a frequent point loss.

Documents checklist (week 4)

  • Valid ID / passport (name matches the booking).
  • Booking confirmation email from Goethe-Institut.
  • Confirmation of fee payment (printed or on phone).
  • Pencils and eraser as allowed by your test centre.
  • Arrival 20–30 minutes before the start time.

Booking, fees & exam-day logistics

Goethe-Institut runs A1 exams at institutes and licensed test partners worldwide. Booking steps are normally: choose a test centre, register on the centre's page, pay the fee, receive a confirmation email with date and time. Fees differ by country and centre — Goethe does not publish a single global price. Check the registration overview and your local institute's page for the current fee in your country. Always confirm the fee, date, and accepted ID before paying.

If your exam is part of a visa application, book only after the German mission has confirmed A1 is required for your route and that Goethe-Zertifikat A1 is on the accepted list. Booking before confirming risks paying for a certificate your case does not need — see our family-reunion language requirement guide.
