30-day Goethe A1 study plan for family reunion

This is a practical 30-day plan for beginners who need German A1 proof for a Germany family reunion timeline. It is not a guarantee of passing. It is a structured way to practise all four skills while keeping official exam and document deadlines visible.

Quick answer

Use 30 days to build routines, not to chase shortcuts.

A safer plan touches speaking, listening, reading, and writing every week. It also checks booking seats, result timing, retake risk, and document deadlines. If your visa appointment is close, do not study in isolation: verify the accepted exam and certificate timing first.

  • Use the German A1 decision helper if you are not sure whether A1 applies.
  • Confirm accepted exam options before paying for a seat.
  • Record your target exam date, expected result date, and visa appointment or document deadline.
  • Download or bookmark official Goethe A1 practice information.
  • Set a daily study block that you can actually keep for 30 days.

Weekly plan

Adjust the pace to your starting level and appointment risk.
Days Main focus Output by the end
1-7 Alphabet, pronunciation, numbers, greetings, personal details, family vocabulary. A 30-second self-introduction and a basic personal vocabulary list.
8-14 Daily routine, time, shopping, appointments, simple forms, short messages. Ten short written sentences and five simple question-answer exchanges.
15-21 Listening for familiar words, reading short notices, speaking requests, weak grammar repair. Three recorded speaking drills and a list of repeated mistakes.
22-27 Official format check, mixed practice, timed short tasks, document and booking review. A realistic exam-week plan and a confirmed document checklist.
28-30 Light review, sleep, ID check, route to test center, exam-day instructions. Ready-to-carry ID, confirmation, route plan, and calm review notes.

Daily routine

Keep each study day small enough to repeat. A tired beginner usually benefits more from consistent short practice than from one heroic weekend.

Block Time Task
Vocabulary 15 minutes Review 15-25 words connected to one life topic.
Listening or reading 20 minutes Use beginner material and write down only the words you can actually reuse.
Speaking 15 minutes Record one answer, listen once, and fix one problem.
Writing 10 minutes Write three short sentences or one tiny message.
Official check 5 minutes Once or twice a week, check booking, result, or document timing.

Risk checks

  • If your appointment is within 30 days, check result timing before increasing study hours.
  • If you have no exam seat, compare official test-center options before making a study schedule too rigid.
  • If speaking is weak, practise short flexible answers instead of memorizing long scripts.
  • If writing is weak, practise forms and short messages rather than advanced grammar.
  • If you fail a practice task repeatedly, repair one pattern at a time.

FAQ

Study planning questions for family reunion applicants.

Can I start this plan from zero?

Yes, but be realistic. If you start from zero and your appointment is close, you may need teacher support, more time, or a retake plan.

Should I study only speaking?

No. Goethe A1 preparation should not ignore listening, reading, and writing. A balanced plan also helps speaking because you reuse the same everyday language.

Should I use leaked materials?

No. Use official samples and original practice. Leaked or copied real exam content is risky, unreliable, and not a durable way to prepare.
