Goethe A1 speaking topics for family reunion

This page gives safe, original speaking practice for Goethe A1 learners. It does not copy leaked questions, real exam cards, or unofficial answer keys. Use it to build everyday German fluency, then compare your preparation with official Goethe information.

Quick answer

Practise simple real-life speaking, not memorized leaked questions.

At A1, your priority is short, clear communication: who you are, where you live, your family, time, appointments, shopping, daily routines, and polite requests. For family reunion applicants, this matters because exam timing is often connected to document deadlines. Safe practice should be original and repeatable.

Safe topic areas

Topic area What to practise Useful A1 language
Introduction Name, age, country, city, language, work, family status. Ich heisse..., Ich komme aus..., Ich wohne in...
Family Spouse, children, parents, simple descriptions, where people live. Mein Mann..., Meine Frau..., Wir haben...
Appointments Dates, times, rescheduling, arriving late, asking for help. Wann ist der Termin? Kann ich bitte...?
Shopping and services Prices, opening hours, quantities, payment, polite requests. Was kostet das? Ich moechte...
Daily routine Morning, work, transport, meals, free time, simple frequency. Ich stehe um..., Ich fahre mit...

Original practice prompts

These are practice prompts, not real exam questions.

One-minute introduction

Say your name, where you are from, where you live, your family situation, and one thing you do every day.

Family question exchange

Ask and answer three simple questions about family members, city, job, or daily life.

Appointment request

Ask for an appointment, ask about time, and politely say that you need help with a document.

Shopping task

Ask for a price, say how many items you want, and ask whether card payment is possible.

Route and transport

Ask where the station is, say how you travel, and ask when the bus or train arrives.

Simple problem

Say you do not understand, ask someone to repeat, and ask whether they can speak slowly.

Speaking templates

Keep your answers short and flexible. Do not memorize one long script that collapses when the question changes.

Situation Simple structure Practice note
Introduce yourself Name + country + city + family + daily activity. Practise 30 seconds first, then 60 seconds.
Ask for help Excuse me + request + thank you. Use polite phrases until they feel automatic.
Answer a question Short answer + one extra detail. Avoid long memorized answers with advanced grammar you cannot control.

Common mistakes

These habits make speaking practice weaker.

Looking for leaked real questions

Leaked content can be inaccurate, unethical, and fragile. Build A1 communication skill with original prompts and official materials.

Practising only introductions

You also need simple requests, questions, dates, prices, and repair phrases such as asking someone to repeat.

Using sentences you cannot adapt

A short correct sentence is usually more useful than a memorized sentence that you cannot change under pressure.
