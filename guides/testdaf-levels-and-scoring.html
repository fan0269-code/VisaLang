TestDaF levels and how scoring works

TestDaF reports your result as TDN 3, 4, or 5 in each of four sections. This page explains the scoring framework so you can read your result and plan the next step.

The TDN scale

TDN 3 means you can understand and produce simple, clear German on familiar academic topics — roughly aligned with CEFR B2. TDN 4 means you handle moderately complex academic language. TDN 5 means you can follow and discuss demanding academic content — aligned with CEFR C1.

How the four sections are scored

Reading, listening, writing, and speaking are each scored separately on the TDN 3–5 scale. You receive a certificate showing four scores. If one section is below the university's threshold, you may be able to retake that section or submit an alternative proof — check with the university.
