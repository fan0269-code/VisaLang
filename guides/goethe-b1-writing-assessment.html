Goethe B1 writing assessment

How the Goethe-Zertifikat B1 writing (Schreiben) module is assessed — the scoring dimensions, the most common point-loss patterns, formal and informal email templates, and a self-check routine. Study aid, not a replacement for official Goethe practice materials.

TL;DR verdict

B1 writing usually asks for a short email or letter plus a guided opinion text. Scoring typically looks at task fulfilment (did you cover all bullet points?), coherence (structure and connectors), vocabulary range, and grammatical accuracy. Most lost marks come from missing a bullet point or using the wrong register (formal "Sie" vs informal "du"). Confirm the current task types and criteria on the official Goethe B1 page.

Who this is for / who should skip

Best for

  • Learners who lose writing marks for missing bullet points or register errors.
  • Self-studiers who need a self-check routine without a tutor.
  • Applicants following the 60-day B1 plan who want deeper writing weeks.

Skip if

  • You already write clean B1 emails covering all bullet points — focus on speaking.
  • You are still at A1 — start with the A1 writing frame in the 30-day A1 plan.

Scoring dimensions

DimensionWhat it means
Task fulfilmentAll bullet points addressed; appropriate length and register.
CoherenceLogical structure; connectors (und, aber, weil, dass).
VocabularyRange appropriate to B1 topics (work, health, travel, media).
GrammarVerb position, cases, tense accuracy.

Exact weights can change — verify on the official page.

Common point-loss patterns

PatternFix
Missing a bullet pointTick each bullet on the prompt as you write it; re-check before submitting.
Wrong register (du vs Sie)Default to "Sie" unless the prompt clearly signals informal.
Missing Betreff (subject line)Write the subject line first, always.
Run-on sentencesSplit into short clauses with connectors.
Forgotten greeting / sign-offUse the formal-email frame; it includes both.

Formal & informal email templates

Formal (default)

Betreff: ______
Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren,
ich schreibe Ihnen wegen ______.
Ich möchte ______ (Anfrage / Beschwerde / Termin).
Können Sie mir ______ mitteilen?
Vielen Dank im Voraus.
Mit freundlichen Grüßen
______ (Ihr Name)

Informal (only if prompt signals it)

Betreff: ______
Hallo ______,
ich ______ (was / wann / wo).
Kannst du ______?
Viele Grüße
______ (Dein Name)
