Quick answer

If your route is Germany family reunion, treat A1 as a serious item to verify before booking.

Some family reunion applicants may need basic German proof, and some cases may be handled differently because of route, status, qualification, health, nationality, or authority-specific checks. Start with the German mission or responsible authority, then verify the accepted exam owner, certificate level, document timing, and booking rules.

Decision path

Step Question What to do
1 Is this a Germany family reunion route? If yes, check the family reunion language rule first. If no, use a route-specific official source.
2 Who is the person in Germany? Record citizenship, residence status, and any skilled-worker or special route details before asking about A1.

Fees, payment rules, seats, and refund policies can change quickly. Use fee articles only as a workflow; use official pages for the actual number.
