# VisaLang PM Audit

Date: 2026-06-30

## Executive Summary

VisaLang is a good first MVP: one static page, clear value proposition, bilingual UI, and a useful first tool. It is not ready for public launch as a business yet because content verification, email capture, analytics, legal pages, and deployment are still missing.

Recommended next move: launch a small validation version focused on one niche, not the whole 50-exam database.

## Current Status

- Product: static bilingual landing page and tool surface.
- Audience: people preparing language exams for visa, residency, citizenship, or work-registration paths.
- Core promise: help users identify the right exam path before buying prep.
- Data: 50 exam seeds and source links, but not all have page-level verified fees, dates, requirements, and retake rules.
- Tech: no framework, no backend, no database, no form integration.

## What Is Good

- Strong brand name: VisaLang is short and expandable.
- Clear first action: Find Your Exam Path is now above the fold.
- Trust posture is correct: official-first, safe prep, no copied real exam questions.
- Bilingual mode is useful for the site owner and early Chinese-speaking operators.
- Static stack is simple enough for a beginner to maintain.

## Biggest Gaps

- No domain, hosting, analytics, or search-console setup.
- Waitlist form is not connected to a real email platform.
- No individual SEO pages yet; current page is a hub.
- No content operations workflow for verifying official sources.
- No privacy policy, terms, contact route, or affiliate disclosure.
- No monetization partner list or outreach pipeline.

## Recommended MVP Scope

Start with one niche:

1. Germany family reunion language exams.
2. Italian/Dutch/Portuguese citizenship language exams.
3. UKVI English exams.

Recommendation: start with Germany family reunion because the user intent is narrow, urgent, and easy to explain.

## Product Roadmap

### Phase 1: Validation

- Publish the homepage.
- Add 10 official-source guide pages.
- Add one real email capture integration.
- Track search queries and waitlist signups.
- Success target: 100 organic visits or 20 waitlist signups.

### Phase 2: Content Engine

- Expand to 50 guide pages.
- Add Last Updated and official source links to every page.
- Add fee/date/format comparison tables.
- Add a simple internal checklist before publishing each page.

### Phase 3: Monetization

- Add tutor/course/consultant lead forms.
- Add original speaking-card PDFs.
- Add AI mock practice only after the source and compliance workflow is stable.

## Risk Register

- Accuracy risk: exam requirements change. Mitigation: official links and Last Updated dates.
- Compliance risk: users may expect official authority. Mitigation: independent-site disclaimer.
- SEO risk: broad keywords are competitive. Mitigation: long-tail country + exam + goal pages.
- Trust risk: no author or contact page. Mitigation: add About, Contact, Privacy, and source policy.
- Conversion risk: form is not connected. Mitigation: integrate email before launch.

## Definition Of Ready For Public Launch

- Domain connected.
- Email form connected and tested.
- Analytics installed.
- Search Console configured.
- At least 10 verified guide pages live.
- Privacy, terms, disclaimer, and contact pages exist.
- Every guide has official sources and Last Updated.
