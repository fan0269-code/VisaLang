# Netherlands Staatsexamen NT2 official-source review — 2026-07-19

## Review scope and decision

Target page:

- `src/content/guides/staatsexamen-nt2-for-work-and-higher-education.md`

This review uses only current first-party sources owned by DUO, the College voor Toetsen en Examens / Staatsexamens Nt2, and one named receiving higher-education institution: the University of Amsterdam (UvA). All sources were accessed on **2026-07-19**.

Overall decision: **PARTIAL SOURCE PACKAGE COMPLETE; MIXED WORK/STUDY PAGE REMAINS BLOCKED**.

- The official exam sources support the identity of the exam, the Programme I/B1 and Programme II/B2 design levels, the four examination components, and the conditions under which DUO issues the diploma.
- The UvA admissions page closes one bounded higher-education example: for the page's stated scope of international applicants to Dutch-taught bachelor's programmes, UvA requires NT2-II when the applicant does not hold one of the listed alternative proofs/exemptions.
- The UvA source does not prove acceptance by every UvA programme, another university, an HBO institution, or an MBO institution. It also does not make language proof sufficient for admission by itself.
- No named employer or professional regulator was identified in this review. Work-route and professional-registration acceptance therefore remain unresolved. Exam-owner descriptions of the level for which a programme is designed must not be rewritten as a named employer's acceptance decision.
- The target page was not edited in this review. Its cautious current body does not promise acceptance, but the page's combined work-and-higher-education scope is not fully closed. Keep `contentStatus: "verification-pending"` unless a later content window narrows the page to the reviewed UvA case or adds a current named employer/regulator source.

## Primary official-source matrix

### S1 — DUO: exam identity, programme levels, and components

- **Owner:** Dienst Uitvoering Onderwijs (DUO)
- **URL:** [Wat is het staatsexamen Nt2?](https://www.duo.nl/particulier/staatsexamen-nt2/hoe-het-staatsexamen-nt2-werkt.jsp)
- **Accessed:** 2026-07-19
- **Page-location clues:** `Wat is het staatsexamen Nt2?` → `Waarom een Nt2-diploma?` → `Programma I en II`
- **Supported facts:** Staatsexamen Nt2 is a national exam for adults learning Dutch as a second language; DUO administers it on behalf of the College voor Toetsen en Examens (CvTE); Programme I is B1 and is designed for MBO study or work at MBO-3/MBO-4 level; Programme II is B2 and is designed for study or work at HBO/university level; both programmes contain Reading, Listening, Speaking, and Writing and are computer-based.
- **Does not support:** acceptance by a particular school, university, employer, or professional regulator; automatic admission or hiring; an applicant-specific choice between Programme I and II.
- **Allowed wording:** “DUO describes Programme I as B1 and aimed at MBO-3/MBO-4 study or work, and Programme II as B2 and aimed at HBO/university-level study or work.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** “Programme I is accepted by every MBO institution” or “Programme II qualifies you for every Dutch university or higher-level job.”

### S2 — Staatsexamens Nt2: programme design and diploma boundary

- **Owner:** College voor Toetsen en Examens / Staatsexamens Nt2
- **URL:** [Wat is het staatsexamen Nt2?](https://www.staatsexamensnt2.nl/over-het-staatsexamen-nt2/wat-is-het-staatsexamen-nt2)
- **Accessed:** 2026-07-19
- **Page-location clues:** `Programma I en II` → `Programma I (B1)` → `Programma II (B2)` → `Examenonderdelen` → `Diploma staatsexamen Nt2`
- **Supported facts:** candidates do not have to take Programme I before Programme II; Programme I is comparable to CEFR B1 and its tasks use MBO-3/MBO-4 study, work, and daily-life contexts; Programme II is comparable to CEFR B2 and its tasks use HBO/university-level study, work, and daily-life contexts; the exam has four components; the diploma is issued after all four components are passed.
- **Does not support:** that an institution accepts four separate certificates instead of the diploma; a receiving organisation's document-format, recency, verification, deadline, or minimum-score rule; universal admission or job eligibility.
- **Allowed wording:** “The official exam site says Programme I and Programme II are separate choices; Programme I does not have to be taken first. A Staatsexamen Nt2 diploma is awarded after all four components are passed.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** “Passing one component proves that you meet a university's Dutch requirement” or “all receiving organisations accept the digital extract or separate certificates.”

### S3 — Staatsexamens Nt2: receiving schools set their own requirements

- **Owner:** College voor Toetsen en Examens / Staatsexamens Nt2
- **URL:** [Hoe gebruik je het diploma Nt2?](https://www.staatsexamensnt2.nl/over-het-staatsexamen-nt2/hoe-gebruik-je-het-diploma)
- **Accessed:** 2026-07-19
- **Page-location clues:** `Opleiding of werk` → `Opleiding` → `Werk`
- **Supported facts:** schools and universities set their own requirements for new students; the candidate should ask the receiving school or university which requirements and diplomas apply; the Staatsexamen Nt2 diploma does not automatically allow a person to start a course of study.
- **Does not support:** which specific institution accepts Programme I or II; a general rule that all universities require NT2-II; admission after language proof alone; a named employer's or regulator's acceptance decision.
- **Allowed wording:** “The official exam owner warns that schools and universities set their own entry requirements and that an NT2 diploma does not automatically confer admission.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** “NT2-II is the Dutch university admission certificate” or “an NT2 diploma guarantees entry once the language condition is met.”

### S4 — DUO: results, diploma, component retakes, and digital records

- **Owner:** Dienst Uitvoering Onderwijs (DUO)
- **URL:** [Uitslag](https://www.duo.nl/particulier/staatsexamen-nt2/uitslag.jsp)
- **Accessed:** 2026-07-19
- **Page-location clues:** `Wanneer krijgt u een diploma?` → `Diploma's en certificaten terugvinden` → `Gezakt?`
- **Supported facts:** DUO issues the diploma when all four components have been passed under the conditions stated on the page; post-2011 diplomas and certificates are available in `Mijn diploma's`; a free digital extract has the same value as the certificate or diploma in DUO's records; a failed component can be retaken.
- **Does not support:** that a particular receiving institution accepts the digital extract, a component certificate, an older record, or a later submission; a universal recency or verification rule; any admission or employment decision.
- **Allowed wording:** “DUO explains how results and the diploma are recorded and says failed components may be retaken.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** “Every university or employer accepts a Mijn diploma's extract” or “component certificates are sufficient for admission.”

### S5 — University of Amsterdam: named higher-education acceptance example

- **Owner / receiving institution:** University of Amsterdam (UvA)
- **URL:** [Dutch language requirements (for Dutch-taught Bachelor's)](https://www.uva.nl/en/education/admissions/bachelors/dutch-taught-programmes/prior-education-non-dutch/dutch-language-requirements/dutch-language-requirements.html)
- **Accessed:** 2026-07-19
- **Page-location clues:** page title `Dutch language requirements (for Dutch-taught Bachelor's)` → opening admissions statement → `Duration of the course` → `Proof of Dutch language proficiency`
- **Supported facts:** within this page's scope, international students need Dutch-language proof before admission to a Dutch-taught degree programme; the page names NT2-II as the required state examination; it lists alternative documents that exempt the applicant from the NT2 examination; if the applicant lacks those listed documents, the page says the applicant must take NT2-II.
- **Does not support:** admission to an English-taught programme; acceptance by another university, HBO, MBO, employer, or regulator; automatic admission to UvA after passing NT2-II; that every applicant lacks an exemption; acceptance of Programme I; a universal document format, validity period, or submission deadline.
- **Allowed wording:** “For the UvA Dutch-taught bachelor's admissions scope described on this page, applicants without one of the listed alternative proofs are required to take Staatsexamen NT2 Programme II.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** “All Dutch universities require NT2-II,” “UvA accepts NT2-II for every programme and applicant,” or “passing NT2-II guarantees UvA admission.”

### S6 — Staatsexamens Nt2: generic work context, not a named employer decision

- **Owner:** College voor Toetsen en Examens / Staatsexamens Nt2
- **URL:** [Werken en het staatsexamen Nt2](https://www.staatsexamensnt2.nl/over-het-staatsexamen-nt2/werken)
- **Accessed:** 2026-07-19
- **Page-location clues:** `Werken en het staatsexamen Nt2` → `Taaltrainingen en cursussen` → `Verplicht stellen?`
- **Supported facts:** the exam owner describes the diploma as evidence of language proficiency; it describes Programme I and II by work/study level and says an employer may choose to require the diploma or make language-training arrangements.
- **Does not support:** that a particular employer requires or accepts NT2; that NT2 is legally mandatory for a job; hiring, salary, promotion, or professional-registration eligibility; the language level required for a named occupation.
- **Allowed wording:** “The exam owner presents NT2 as evidence of language proficiency that an employer may choose to use; candidates must check the named employer's actual requirement.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** “Dutch employers require NT2,” “NT2-II is accepted for professional jobs,” or “a candidate without NT2 must still be considered by every employer.”

The current target page is deliberately minimal and does not state a specific employer, regulator, institution, accepted programme, score, or document format.

- The statement that the page has not yet identified a reviewed institution, employer, or professional regulator for the combined page scope.

## Remaining blockers

1. **Work route:** no named Dutch employer's recruitment or language-requirement page was reviewed. The work branch cannot be promoted from exam-purpose language to an acceptance fact.
2. **Professional route:** no named Dutch professional regulator was selected. Regulated-profession eligibility, level, exemptions, and alternative proofs remain unknown.
3. **MBO/HBO breadth:** no named MBO or university of applied sciences admissions page was reviewed. Programme I/MBO and Programme II/HBO mappings remain exam-design descriptions, not proven institutional acceptance.
4. **Reader-specific UvA decision:** the UvA page establishes a bounded admissions rule, but not the reader's diploma admissibility, exemption status, programme-specific conditions, deadline, or final admission outcome.
5. **Submission mechanics:** the reviewed UvA page does not close whether a DUO digital extract or separate certificates are accepted, nor any recency or verification procedure. These must remain questions for UvA or the selected receiver.
