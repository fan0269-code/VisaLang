The reader's **competent authority** controls settlement-permit, citizenship, work, study, and other receiving-body acceptance questions. The selected **official or authorised test centre** controls current exam product, booking, identification, fee, result, certificate, cancellation, and rescheduling instructions. When those instructions are local, time-sensitive, unavailable, or conflicting, the guide must preserve a named reader-side verification action instead of converting a general official page into a conclusion.

## Editorial guardrails for follow-up work

- Do not add fixed fees, result times, retake windows, appointment waits, accepted-certificate guarantees, exemption conclusions, or individual eligibility findings from general official pages.
- Keep the reader journey: competent authority -> accepted proof -> exact exam product -> official or authorised centre -> checklist -> timeline -> Route Review boundary.
- Keep B1 language proof, civic knowledge, residence history, livelihood/income, insurance, housing, identity, criminal-history treatment, documents, forms, fees, appointments, and procedure as separate evidence categories.
- Do not create additional B1 pages unless the existing 13-page cluster cannot serve a distinct search intent.
