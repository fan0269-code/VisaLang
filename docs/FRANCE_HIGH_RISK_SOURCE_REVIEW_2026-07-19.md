## Source matrix — residence, nationality, and TCF IRN

All sources in this section were accessed on 2026-07-19.

### R1. Residence-card language requirements

- **Official source:** [Service-Public — Carte de résident et carte de séjour pluriannuelle : comment justifier de votre connaissance du français ?](https://www.service-public.gouv.fr/particuliers/vosdroits/F34501)
- **Page metadata:** verified 2026-02-10 by the Direction de l'information légale et administrative (Prime Minister).
- **Location cues:** opening summary; `Carte de séjour pluriannuelle`; `Carte de résident`; `Les seuls tests reconnus`; `À noter` on disability/health arrangements and dispensation.
- **Supported facts:** since 2026-01-01, some first applications by non-European nationals require proof of French; the current general levels are A2 for covered first multi-year residence cards and B1 for covered first resident cards; not every card is covered; for the covered resident-card cases the page identifies age/category limits; recognised tests are TCF and TEF; linguistic-test attestations are valid for two years and have identity/photo/security requirements; health/disability arrangements or dispensation may exist through the prescribed medical process.
- **Not supported:** “every residence permit requires TCF IRN”; “all applicants need A2/B1”; “TCF IRN is the only proof”; an individual exception; renewal rules inferred from a first-application rule; a prefecture's acceptance of a particular document without checking the dossier.
- **Allowed wording:** “Since 1 January 2026, covered first applications for a multi-year residence card or resident card may require A2 or B1 respectively. The Service-Public page must be checked for the exact card and applicant category.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** “TCF IRN is mandatory for French residence” or “A2/B1 applies to every residence route.”

### R2. Legal categories of proof and validity for covered residence cards

- **Official source:** [Légifrance — Order of 22 December 2025 on proof of French proficiency for multi-year and resident cards](https://www.legifrance.gouv.fr/loda/id/JORFTEXT000053164506/2025-12-27)
- **Page metadata:** in force from 2026-01-01; consolidated data current on the reviewed page.
- **Location cues:** Article 1 (A2 diplomas); Article 2 (A2 certification criteria); Article 3 (B1 diplomas); Article 4 (B1 certification criteria); Article 5 (attestation security and two-year validity); Article 7 (entry into force).
- **Supported facts:** qualifying proof can include specified French/state/professional diplomas and any diploma attesting at least A2 or B1 as applicable; certifications must cover distinct oral/written expression and comprehension tests under the specified conditions; relevant linguistic attestations are valid for two years.
- **Not supported:** automatic acceptance of any document merely labelled “B1” or “B2”; that every DELF level is sufficient for every card; that the lifetime validity of a DELF diploma removes the need to satisfy the exact proof and route rules.
- **Allowed wording:** “The implementing order recognises diploma and certification categories, with different A2/B1 thresholds for the covered routes.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** “Only a test is accepted” or “a lifetime diploma guarantees the residence card.”

### R3. Named recognised linguistic tests for covered residence cards

- **Official source:** [Légifrance — Order of 22 December 2025 naming recognised linguistic certifications](https://www.legifrance.gouv.fr/jorf/id/JORFTEXT000053164540)
- **Page metadata:** in force from 2026-01-01.
- **Location cues:** Article 1.
- **Supported facts:** the named linguistic certifications are TCF delivered by FEI and TEF delivered by the Paris Chamber of Commerce and Industry.
- **Not supported:** that a reader must choose TCF over TEF; that every TCF variant or score sheet is suitable without checking the procedure; acceptance of a test result outside the other statutory conditions.
- **Allowed wording:** “TCF and TEF are the two named test families in the current implementing order; verify the exact product and result against the receiving procedure.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** “TCF IRN is the sole government-approved route.”

### R4. Current nationality language level and accepted evidence

- **Official source:** [Service-Public — Nationalité française : comment justifier de son niveau en français ?](https://www.service-public.gouv.fr/particuliers/vosdroits/F11926)
- **Page metadata:** verified 2026-01-01.
- **Location cues:** situation selector; `Procédure d'acquisition ... par décret`; `Déclaration ... par mariage`; `quel est le niveau exigé`; `quels documents sont acceptés`; dispensation section.
- **Supported facts:** B2 oral and written is the current language level for naturalisation by decree, reintegration by decree, and declaration by marriage; accepted categories include a French diploma or diploma attesting at least B2, and TCF or TEF success attestations issued less than two years earlier; a disability/health-based dispensation process may apply under the stated conditions.
- **Not supported:** an individual's eligibility; automatic acceptance of a scanned or expired result; any other nationality route not selected on the page; an individual dispensation; nationality as the consequence of passing B2.
- **Allowed wording:** “For the listed procedures, the current language threshold is B2 in oral and written French; B2 proof is one dossier condition, not a nationality decision.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** “Passing TCF IRN B2 grants French citizenship” or “all ways of becoming French require the same proof.”

### R5. Ministry of the Interior nationality procedure

- **Official source:** [Ministry of the Interior, DGEF — Les procédures d'accès à la nationalité française](https://www.immigration.interieur.gouv.fr/devenir-francais/procedures-dacces-a-nationalite-francaise)
- **Location cues:** `Depuis le 1er janvier 2026`; `La naturalisation et la réintégration`; `Justifier d'un niveau en langue française`; `L'examen civique`; `L'entretien en préfecture`.
- **Supported facts:** B2 oral and written applies from 2026-01-01 to naturalisation, reintegration, and declaration by marriage; for naturalisation/reintegration, language evidence is only one part of a dossier and examination process; the prefecture/platform assesses the file; a separate civic examination applies to naturalisation/reintegration.
- **Not supported:** a favourable decision from possession of TCF/DELF; extending the civic examination to declaration by marriage; relying on the stale embedded explanatory-video transcript on the same page, which still mentions B1.
- **Allowed wording:** “The Ministry's current operative text states B2. A TCF/DELF result does not replace the rest of the procedure or the authority's decision.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** quoting the page's stale embedded B1 transcript as the current rule.

### R6. Civic examination is separate and procedure-specific

- **Official source:** [Service-Public — Naturalisation française : comment passer l'examen civique ?](https://www.service-public.gouv.fr/particuliers/vosdroits/F39426)
- **Page metadata:** verified 2026-07-16.
- **Location cues:** opening `À savoir`; scope of procedures; programme; dispensation; registration.
- **Supported facts:** the civic examination is separate from language proof; it concerns acquisition by decree (naturalisation and reintegration); the page expressly says declaration procedures, including marriage, are not concerned; a health/disability dispensation process may apply.
- **Not supported:** that TCF IRN includes or replaces the civic examination; that marriage declaration applicants must take the civic examination; an individual's dispensation.
- **Allowed wording:** “TCF IRN evaluates language. The civic examination is a separate requirement for the decree procedures identified by Service-Public.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** “TCF IRN covers both the French test and the civic test.”

### R7. TCF IRN current French product facts

- **Official source:** [France Éducation international — TCF Intégration, Résidence et Nationalité](https://www.france-education-international.fr/test/tcf-irn)
- **Location cues:** opening intended-use list; `Présentation des épreuves`; `Inscription`; `Résultats`.
- **Supported facts:** the current French page positions TCF IRN for nationality, long-term residence, multi-year residence, and A1 validation in the OFII pathway; it has four mandatory general-French tests: listening (25 questions, 20 minutes), reading (25 questions, 35 minutes), writing (3 exercises, 30 minutes), and speaking (3 exercises, face-to-face, 10 minutes); total duration is 1 hour 35 minutes; it evaluates language only, not French history/culture/society; it evaluates up to B2; FEI transmits results to the test centre within 15 working days after FEI receives the session material; the centre gives the candidate the attestation; the attestation is valid for two years from the result-issue date; the selected authorised centre controls dates, registration method, delivery medium, and price.
- **Not supported:** applicability to the reader's exact procedure; a result deadline counted directly from test day; a universal fee/session; prefecture acceptance; civic-exam completion.
- **Allowed wording:** “TCF IRN is FEI's four-skill language test up to B2. Check the receiving procedure first, then obtain local dates, fee and delivery terms from the selected authorised centre.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** “TCF IRN is required for every residence/citizenship case” or “results arrive 15 working days after the test.”

### R8. TCF IRN 2025 format change and source conflict

- **Official source:** [France Éducation international — Le TCF IRN évolue](https://www.france-education-international.fr/actualites/nouveautes-tcf-irn)
- **Page metadata:** published 2025-03-31; change effective 2025-05-12.
- **Location cues:** `Le nouveau TCF IRN`; old/new format table.
- **Supported facts:** the product was expanded to B2 from 2025-05-12; listening and reading changed from the old format and the total duration changed from 1 hour 15 minutes to 1 hour 35 minutes.
- **Not supported / conflict:** FEI's currently indexed English TCF IRN page still displays the old B1/1-hour-15 format. Official FEI indexed content also exposes both **20 days** and **30 days** as a wait between TCF IRN sittings. This review does not choose between those two intervals.
- **Allowed wording:** use the current French primary product page for B2/1-hour-35 format; say “recheck the current waiting period directly with FEI and the authorised centre before rebooking.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** publishing either 20 or 30 days as settled while FEI's first-party pages remain inconsistent; using the stale English format.

## Source matrix — DELF/DALF, study, work, and professional regulation

All sources in this section were accessed on 2026-07-19.

### D1. DELF product facts

- **Official source:** [France Éducation international — DELF tout public](https://www.france-education-international.fr/diplome/delf-tout-public?langue=fr)
- **Location cues:** `Présentation générale`; `Publics concernés`; `Niveaux évalués`.
- **Supported facts:** DELF tout public is an official French Ministry of Education diploma; its independent levels are A1, A2, B1, and B2; the diploma itself is valid for life.
- **Not supported:** universal university admission; employer acceptance; professional registration; a current residence/nationality decision; that a receiving organisation cannot impose its own evidence or application rules.
- **Allowed wording:** “The DELF diploma itself is valid for life and certifies the stated CEFR level.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** “DELF B2 is accepted forever by every university/employer/authority” or FEI's broad marketing phrase as proof of a final decision.

### D2. DALF product facts

- **Official source:** [France Éducation international — DALF](https://www.france-education-international.fr/diplome/dalf?langue=fr)
- **Location cues:** `Présentation générale`; `Niveaux évalués`.
- **Supported facts:** DALF is an official French Ministry of Education diploma; its independent levels are C1 and C2; the diploma itself is valid for life.
- **Not supported:** a DALF requirement for all master's programmes; automatic admission; employer or regulator acceptance.
- **Allowed wording:** “DALF C1/C2 are lifetime diplomas; the target programme or authority still decides what it requires.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** “DALF C1 is universally required for French master's study.”

### D3. Bounded DAP exemption for DELF B2

- **Official source:** [France Éducation international — Demande d'admission préalable / TCF DAP](https://www.france-education-international.fr/article/tcf-dap)
- **Location cues:** `Présentation générale`; `Dispenses`; 2026 intake calendar.
- **Supported facts:** in the page's DAP scope (first-year licence or national architecture-school study), DELF B2 and DALF C1/C2 holders are listed among those exempt from the TCF language examination used in the DAP process.
- **Not supported:** exemption from DAP itself in every case; admission; acceptance for another programme/year; a France-wide language rule; any 2027 intake date.
- **Source-quality warning:** the current 2026 calendar text contains an apparent year inconsistency in one commission-date line. No deadline from this page should be republished without checking the live intake notice and receiving institution.
- **Allowed wording:** “For the current DAP scope, FEI lists DELF B2 and DALF C1/C2 among TCF-exempt credentials; this is a test exemption, not an admission decision.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** “DELF B2 guarantees entry to a French university.”

### D4. Current government DAP procedure boundary

- **Official source:** [Service-Public — Dossier vert, demande d'admission préalable 2026–2027](https://www.service-public.gouv.fr/particuliers/vosdroits/R1278)
- **Page metadata:** verified 2025-12-15; cycle-specific to 2026–2027.
- **Location cues:** audience/scope; `Étapes de la démarche`; `Et après ?`.
- **Supported facts:** the DAP file is a defined first-year university process for its stated non-EU/EEA/Swiss audience; a TCF may be scheduled when the applicant is not exempt; the universities notify their admission responses.
- **Not supported:** DELF B1 as a DAP exemption; later-year/master admission; a future-cycle deadline; admission merely because the language condition is met.
- **Allowed wording:** “Language evidence is one node in a cycle- and applicant-specific admission procedure; the universities issue the admission responses.”
- **Forbidden generalisation:** using a DAP calendar as a universal French-university application calendar.

### D5. Named receiving institution example — Sorbonne University Faculty of Arts and Humanities

- **Allowed wording:** “At Sorbonne University's Faculty of Arts and Humanities, the reviewed page states B2 for licence and C1 for master's applications. This is a named-faculty example, not a national rule.”

### D6. Ordinary foreign-worker language boundary

- **Official source:** [Service-Public — Un salarié non européen qui s'installe en France doit-il connaître le français ?](https://www.service-public.gouv.fr/particuliers/vosdroits/F17450)

### D7. Named regulated-profession example — physicians

## Remaining blockers and required next evidence

1. **Exact residence route not selected.** The page still lacks the reader's named card, first-application/renewal state, nationality/agreement context, age, and any claimed exception. A prefecture or the exact Service-Public procedure must close those facts.
2. **Individual dispensation cannot be pre-decided.** Health/disability arrangements and dispensations require the prescribed certificate and the instructing authority's process.
3. **FEI retake interval conflict.** Current first-party indexed pages expose both 20 and 30 days. Do not publish either until FEI's live French page/manual and the selected centre confirm the same value.
4. **FEI English TCF IRN page is stale.** It still exposes the old B1/1h15 product. The current French page and 2025 change notice support B2/1h35; English copy should not be used until synchronised.
5. **No local TCF/DELF centre selected.** Dates, fee, seat, ID, cancellation, transfer, result handover, and document delivery remain unknown.
6. **Only one bounded university example is reviewed.** The Sorbonne University Faculty of Arts and Humanities example cannot close another faculty, programme, year, or institution. The target programme's current admission page is still required.
7. **DAP dates are cycle-specific and one FEI line is internally inconsistent.** No deadline should be copied into evergreen content without a live-cycle check.
8. **No named employer is reviewed.** The government worker page does not establish employer acceptance or a universal DELF threshold.
9. **Only the physician regulator is reviewed.** Nurses, pharmacists, lawyers, teachers, and other regulated professions need their own named competent-authority pages; the physician rule cannot be transferred.
10. **No outcome claim is supportable.** Neither a language diploma nor test result proves admission, employment, professional registration, residence approval, nationality acquisition, or an individual exemption.
