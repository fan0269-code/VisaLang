If you work full-time and need to prepare for the Goethe A1 exam, this 8-week study plan is designed for you. It assumes 1-2 hours of study per day on weekdays and slightly longer sessions on weekends.

## Before you start

- Confirm which exam you need (Goethe-Zertifikat A1: Start Deutsch 1 for adults).
- Download the official Goethe practice materials from the Goethe-Institut website.
- Get a basic A1 German textbook or app that covers vocabulary, grammar, and all four skills.
- Set up a regular study time that fits your work schedule.

## Week 1-2: Foundation

**Focus**: Basic vocabulary, pronunciation, and simple sentence structures.

- Learn 15-20 new words per day using flashcards or a vocabulary app.
- Practice basic greetings, introductions, and personal information.
- Learn the German alphabet and basic pronunciation rules.
- Practice typing and writing simple German sentences.

**Weekend work** (2-3 hours each day):
- Review all vocabulary from the week.
- Record yourself speaking and compare to native audio.
- Practice simple dialogues: introducing yourself, asking and answering basic questions.

## Week 3-4: Building blocks

**Focus**: Grammar foundations and listening comprehension.

- Learn basic verb conjugation (present tense, regular verbs).
- Practice sentence structure: subject-verb-object and basic questions.
- Listen to slow German audio for 15 minutes daily (news for learners, simple podcasts).
- Practice reading short texts: signs, simple emails, short descriptions.

**Weekend work**:
- Grammar review: write 10-15 sentences using the structures learned this week.
- Listening practice: listen to a short audio clip 3 times, write down what you understand.
- Start learning numbers, dates, times, and prices in German.

## Week 5-6: Exam skills

**Focus**: Applying your knowledge to the exam format.

- Use official Goethe A1 practice tests to understand the exam format.
- Practice the reading section: short texts with comprehension questions.
- Practice the listening section: short audio clips with multiple-choice or fill-in questions.
- Start writing practice: filling out forms, writing short messages.

**Weekend work**:
- Full practice test: complete a reading and listening section under timed conditions.
- Writing practice: write 3-4 short messages or form-filling exercises.
- Begin speaking practice: self-introduction and responding to simple questions.

## Week 7-8: Exam readiness

**Focus**: Test simulation and weak-area review.

- Take at least two full practice exams under timed conditions.
- Identify which section is your weakest and spend extra time there.
- Speaking practice every day: self-introduction, answering questions, making requests.
- Review all vocabulary and grammar from the first 6 weeks.

**Weekend work (week 7)**:
- Full mock exam: all four sections under exam conditions.
- Speaking simulation: practice with a partner, a tutor, or record yourself.

**Weekend work (week 8 — the final weekend before your exam)**:
- Light review only. Do not try to learn new material.
- Read through your vocabulary flashcards one last time.
- Prepare your documents for exam day.
- Get a good night's sleep.

## Study tips for working adults

- **Consistency over intensity.** 1 hour every day is better than 4 hours on Saturday only.
- **Use commute time.** Listen to German audio during your commute.
- **Micro-sessions count.** 15 minutes of vocabulary during lunch adds up.
- **Do not skip weekends entirely.** Even 1 hour each weekend day keeps momentum.
- **Track your progress.** Mark off each day you studied. Seeing the streak helps motivation.

## What you can realistically achieve

After 8 weeks of consistent 1-2 hour daily study, you should be able to:
- Introduce yourself confidently in German.
- Understand simple spoken German when spoken slowly and clearly.
- Read and understand short, simple texts.
- Write basic messages and fill in simple forms.
- Be familiar with the Goethe A1 exam format and question types.
