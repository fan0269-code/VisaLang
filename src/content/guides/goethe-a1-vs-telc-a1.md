## The first comparison is acceptance

Do not begin with a brand, a nearby classroom, or a lower price. Ask the German mission or authority responsible for your case which certificate it currently accepts for the family reunion route.

If the answer is unclear, request the relevant official page or written clarification. This guide cannot declare that either certificate will be accepted in every country or case.

## Compare the exact product, not just "A1"

For Goethe, identify the adult **Goethe-Zertifikat A1: Start Deutsch 1**. telc offers several German A1 products, so identify the exact local product before comparing it with Goethe.

An A1 course, youth exam, practice product, or centre advertisement is not automatically the exam proof you need. Match the product name to the authority's instruction and the official provider page.

## Use four decision checks

1. **Acceptance:** confirm the exact certificate with the responsible authority.
2. **Availability:** confirm that an official local centre offers that exact exam and a workable date.
3. **Process:** check ID rules, booking deadline, result timing, certificate delivery, and any local cancellation or rescheduling rule.
4. **Total cost:** compare the exam fee together with travel, document timing, and a possible later attempt.

The best option is the one that clears all four checks for your case. A famous provider or a cheaper first price is not enough by itself.

## Avoid false shortcuts

Do not infer acceptance from another applicant, assume two A1 labels are interchangeable, or commit to a visa appointment before you know the result and certificate process. Keep the official source and the date you checked it in your record.
