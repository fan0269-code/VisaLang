## The two exams you need

Spanish citizenship by residence (10 years, or 2 years for some nationalities) requires proof of Spanish language at A2 level and knowledge of Spanish culture and history. These are two separate exams:

- **DELE A2:** Diplomas de Espanol como Lengua Extranjera at A2 level, run by Instituto Cervantes.
- **CCSE:** Conocimientos Constitucionales y Socioculturales de Espana, also run by Instituto Cervantes.

## DELE A2 format

DELE A2 tests reading, listening, writing, and speaking at CEFR A2. It is offered at Instituto Cervantes centres and authorised exam partners worldwide. Sessions are scheduled at fixed dates throughout the year.

## CCSE format

The CCSE is a 45-minute multiple-choice exam covering Spanish government, culture, history, and society. It is offered regularly. You must pass both DELE A2 and CCSE to apply for citizenship.

## What to verify

- Confirm your residence period qualifies you for citizenship before booking exams.
- Check if your nationality has a reduced 2-year residence requirement (some Latin American countries, Sephardic descent).
- Book DELE A2 and CCSE through Instituto Cervantes or an authorised centre.
- Record exam fees, dates, and retake policies from the centre.
