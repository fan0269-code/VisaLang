## The language-proof boundary

Goethe-Zertifikat B1 can be language evidence if the competent authority says it is accepted for your exact settlement-permit route. It does not establish that you qualify for a settlement permit. VisaLang cannot assess an individual permit application, exemption, or outcome.

## Keep B1 separate from the wider permit file

The authority may need to assess conditions beyond language proof. Keep separate records and questions for residence duration or status, livelihood or income, health insurance, housing, identity and civil-status evidence, pension or other route-specific evidence, application forms, fees, appointments and procedure. A certificate provider cannot decide those points.

## What to verify before choosing an exam

- Which authority handles your application and which current route it says applies.
- Whether language proof is required, what exact level or alternative it names, and whether it accepts Goethe B1, telc B1, DTZ, or another proof.
- Which non-language evidence it expects and how it wants documents submitted.
- Only after that: the official local centre's exam date, fee, ID rule, cancellation terms and result process.

## Do not turn a timeline into a legal conclusion

Use the [B1 timeline](/guides/germany-b1-settlement-citizenship-timeline/) to plan a verification, exam and submission buffer. It is not a calculator for residence years, insurance, income, housing or permit eligibility.
