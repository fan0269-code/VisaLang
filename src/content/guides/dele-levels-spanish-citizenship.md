## Official verification required before relying on a level

Start with the [Spanish Ministry of Justice residence-nationality procedure](https://www.mjusticia.gob.es/es/ciudadania/tramites/nacionalidad-residencia). It is the deciding-authority starting point; an exam-provider page alone cannot decide which proof or dispensation applies to your application.

## Questions for the receiving authority

- Which current procedure applies to your citizenship application?
- Is language proof required for your applicant category, and are any exemptions relevant?
- Which certificate, level, version, and document format does the authority accept?
- Does the authority accept SIELE, DELE, another form of proof, or an alternative for your case?

Record the authority page and the date checked before choosing a level or paying for an exam.

## What the reviewed sources can confirm

The Ministry procedure confirms the residence-nationality route, and its electronic office provides a separate path for requesting dispensation from Instituto Cervantes tests. The current Cervantes nationality page describes DELE A2 or higher and CCSE as tests it administers in certain nationality cases. Its DELE A2 page describes the A2 product and format. Together, these sources support the verification sequence; they do not establish your applicant category, accepted evidence, or individual dispensation result.

Use the DELE and SIELE links below to check their current products and registration information only after the Ministry procedure has confirmed which proof applies. SIELE is included as a question to verify, not as an accepted citizenship certificate claim.

## Next action

Confirm the Ministry procedure and accepted evidence before choosing a DELE level or another product. Save the current authority page or written instruction with the date checked; only then use the exam owner's current product and centre information.
